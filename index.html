Campus Life

School Cafeteria

Opened in 1998

From 1998 to 2023, the school cafeteria has served millions of students, tolerated their messes and accidents, and gave them a place to talk and laugh. First opened on the school's first mid year festival, the beautiful cafeteria had shone and sparkle, and served healthy meals for hungry students after their lessons. But after surviving over 20 years of students in 2018, the place needed cleaning up. But with the help of the student council, and the clean up crew, the school was able to make the cafeteria prettier than it's ever been.



School Auditorium

Opened in 1999

In the wonderful school auditorium, we host competitions, debates, and assemblies. We also perform class plays there in winter and spring, and we also watch fun movies there on school movie night. A few years ago, a tragedy happened in our auditorium. There was a fire, a small one, but big enough to destroy half of the place. But this year, we have a newly reinnovated auditorium, that is more wonderful than ever!



School Library

Opened in 2003

Opened on the school's 5th anniversary, the library has always been helpful for both students and teacher. A calm place for kids to relax and learn, and a peaceful place for teachers to plan their lesson plans. Each grade has a special day to visit the library, pick books, and listen to a story read by the librarian.

What's New

  • The Renovated Auditorium

    Who knew faulty Christmas lights could destroy half of our historic 20 year old Auditorium. No one did, in fact, when one peaceful morning, the fire alarm rang crazily. All the students thought it was a normal drill, but our brave janitor, Mr. Mackleroy, knew something was fishy. He spotted a little first grade girl near the fire alarm puller, who is known as our hero, Reyna Backdrop. But before he could confront her, Mr. Mackeroy saw thick black smoke floating from the cracks of the auditorium door. He lifted Reyna, saw the flames, and hurried outside, where the other people were. He notified, the firemen where the fire was, and the fire was put out before it damaged the whole auditorium. But the auditorium looked very damaged, and it wasn't safe to enter. That meant no Christmas performances, or special Christmas movie days. It was a disappointing 2019 Christmas. But thankfully, now, we have renovvated and finally fixed the auditorium, and classrooms around it that got damaged. That means, we will finally have programs for Christmas 2023!



  • Implemented Online Attendance Management System

    A tragedy happened a few years ago to two of the students at our school. Sarah and Wilfred Banks, who lived around 10 minutes from the school, were walking to school, when they were abducted by a man driving a strange sportscar. Their teacher thought that they were sick or they were just absent, but because she knew that they usually walk to school, she checked with the principal. He had got no phone calls from their parents, and quickly contacted them. The worried parents notified the school that the siblings had left the house two hours ago. Immiediatly the school contacted the police, and checked the roadside camera, which had capture the two kids walking two hours ago, and how they had gotten into a sportscar. They found the license plate of the car a day later, and tracked it down to an abandoned cabin at the edge of the town. There the police discovered other kidnapped kids, and returned them to their homes. From that day on, parents walked with their children, and the school, invented a clever system, in which parents will notify the school if their children are absent, and the school will notify the parents when the child arrives at the school, if they are not absent. Otherwise they will call the parents, and then alert the police.
